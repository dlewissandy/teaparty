## Pattern 7: Decision Specificity Reduces Downstream Ambiguity

When delegating tasks, include specific, unambiguous decisions rather than leaving parameters open-ended. Vague delegation leads to inconsistent outputs and rework. The more concrete the task specification (formats, constraints, scope), the fewer clarification loops are needed mid-execution.

## Pattern 8: Handoff Verification Catches Consistency Issues

Before passing work from one stage/team to the next, verify that outputs meet the downstream team's input requirements. A brief boundary-check step (reviewing format, completeness, internal consistency) prevents compounding errors that are costly to unwind later. The specific criteria for verification vary by domain, but the pattern applies universally.
