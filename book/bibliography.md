# Bibliography and Source Notes

**Attardo, Salvatore.** "Humor in Language." *Oxford Research Encyclopedia of Linguistics.* 2017. DOI: 10.1093/acrefore/9780199384655.013.342.

*(Used in Ch6: frame-shifting as the cognitive operation at the punchline)*

**McDowell, John.** "Riddling and Enculturation: A Glance at the Cerebral Child." *Children's Folklore Review.* IU ScholarWorks open access: https://scholarworks.iu.edu/iuswrrest/api/core/bitstreams/dac5b700-fe46-4ec4-82ad-5980955919ea/content

**Morreall, John.** "Philosophy of Humor." *Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy.* First published 2012; revised 2024. https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/humor/

**Norrick, Neal R.** "Issues in Conversational Joking." *Journal of Pragmatics* 35, no. 9 (2003): 1303–1319. DOI: 10.1016/S0378-2166(02)00180-7. Academia.edu: https://www.academia.edu/61963814/Issues_in_conversational_joking

**Palmann, A., and T. Miller.** "What's in a Pun? In *Proceedings of the Annual Meeting of the Cognitive Science Society,* 2006. https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/Computationally-Recognizing-Wordplay-in-Jokes-Rayz-Mazlack/618f5ec452da1586f27c8743170e9bff8a0ac772

**Rose, Spalek, and Abdel Rahman.** "Pun Exposure Induces Co-activation of Phonologically Related Word Meanings in Language Production." *PLoS ONE* 10, no. 7 (2015): e0130853. DOI: 10.1371/journal.pone.0130853. PMC open access: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4482729/

**Schmidt, R. C., et al.** "Bodily Synchronization Underlying Joke Telling." *Frontiers in Human Neuroscience* 8 (2014): Article 633. DOI: 10.3389/fnhum.2014.00633. PMC open access: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4133755/

**USC Digital Folklore Archives.** "Interrupting Cow Knock-Knock Joke." http://folklore.usc.edu/interrupting-cow-knock-knock-joke/

**Weide, Jared.** "The Linguistic Mechanisms of Knock-Knock Jokes." Honors Capstone Project, University of Texas at Arlington, Fall 2014. CC BY 4.0. https://mavmatrix.uta.edu/cgi/viewcontent.cgi?article=1007&context=honors_fall2014

*(Used in Ch1: metafolklore framework; Shakespeare attribution contested; Ben Jonson as alternative)*

**World Journal of Advanced Research and Reviews.** "Developmental Trajectory of Humor and Laughter in Children" (2022). https://wjarr.com/sites/default/files/WJARR-2022-0810.pdf
