# Knock Knock: A History
## Table of Contents

---

**Chapter 1 — "Before Anyone Was Home": Origins and Precursors**
From Shakespeare's drunken porter (a myth, mostly) to Ben Jonson's earlier and more structurally honest knock-knock exchange, to the children's door games and vaudeville wordplay that actually fed the form. What had to exist before the knock-knock joke could exist?

**Chapter 2 — "Knock Knock, Who's There? America": The 1930s Craze**
In the summer of 1936, Americans became obsessed. Newspaper contests, radio songs, commercial party game sets, a cartoonist named Bob Dunn, and the question of how an empty template becomes a national phenomenon.

**Chapter 3 — The Peak and the Groan: Golden Age and Backlash**
Adults declared the joke exhausted by 1937. Children disagreed — and kept the form alive through the children's transmission system that the Opies and the Knapps documented, one school bus at a time. The adult/child divide as the joke's survival mechanism.

**Chapter 4 — On the Air, on the Screen, in the Ad: Knock-Knock in Popular Culture**
Television used the joke as shorthand for innocence; adult comedy used it as shorthand for cliché. Advertising deployed it as a pre-tested participatory frame. The Zimmerman trial deployed it disastrously. Breaking Bad riffed on its authority connotations. The dual register of innocent fun and knowing groan as the engine of a century's worth of media use.

**Chapter 5 — "Who's There? Whoever You Want": The Joke Goes Global and Online**
The knock-knock joke's phonetic mechanism is almost untranslatable — and yet the format spread to dozens of languages. The container traveled without the contents. Online, the container became the joke: anti-jokes, structural inversions, and the Interrupting Cow as documented folk transmission.

**Chapter 6 — Why It Works: The Mechanics of a Perfect Joke Container**
No one has published a peer-reviewed paper specifically studying the knock-knock joke as a genre — and yet every major humor framework maps onto it with suspicious precision. Script theory, conversation analysis, ERP neuroscience, developmental psychology, and the one dedicated undergraduate taxonomy assemble to explain a cultural object that has survived by being too obvious to study.
