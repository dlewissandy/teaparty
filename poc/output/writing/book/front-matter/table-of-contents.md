## Chapters

### 1. What Your Eyes Already Know
*You have been doing astronomy your whole life without realizing it. This chapter begins with the naked-eye sky — the moon, the planets, the constellations — and shows you how to read the night as our ancestors did.*

### 2. Our Cosmic Home
*The solar system is not just the sun and eight planets. It is a vast, buzzing system of moons, asteroids, comets, and icy bodies at its fringes. Discover the shape and scale of the neighborhood closest to home.*

### 3. The Solar System
*A tour of each world in our sun's family: from Mercury's sun-scorched craters to Neptune's supersonic winds, with stops at every remarkable landscape in between.*

### 4. The Star We Call Home
*The sun is not just a light switch in the sky — it is a roiling, magnetically turbulent ball of plasma that shapes all life on Earth. Learn how it works, what it will eventually become, and why we owe it everything.*

### 5. Lives of the Stars
*Stars are born, they live for millions or billions of years, and they die in ways that range from a quiet whisper to a cataclysmic explosion. This chapter traces the full arc of stellar life.*

### 6. Islands of Stars
*Zoom out far enough and the stars cluster into galaxies — enormous, gravity-bound islands, each containing hundreds of billions of suns. Meet the Milky Way and its closest cosmic neighbors.*

### 7. The Big Picture: Cosmology
*Pull back further still and galaxies themselves form filaments and walls around vast empty voids. This chapter takes on the origin, structure, and ultimate fate of the universe itself.*

### 8. Other Worlds: Exoplanets
*In the last three decades, astronomers have discovered thousands of planets orbiting other stars. Some are hell-worlds of molten rock; others sit tantalizingly in the "Goldilocks zone" where liquid water could exist.*

### 9. The Invisible Universe
*Most of the universe cannot be seen. Dark matter holds galaxies together; dark energy is pushing everything apart. This chapter explores what we know — and how strange it is — about the cosmos we cannot directly observe.*

### 10. Are We Alone?
*The final question: given what we now know about the sheer number of worlds out there, is life unique to Earth? This chapter surveys the search for life beyond our planet and asks what an answer — in either direction — would mean.*
